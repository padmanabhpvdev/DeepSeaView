An Overview

Underwater images often suffer from poor visibility due to light absorption, scattering and color distortion. DeepSeaView is an advanced image enhancement system designed to improve the clarity and quality of underwater visuals using Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT). By leveraging the frequency-domain properties of DCT, the system effectively reduces noise, enhances contrast and restores color balance in submerged environments. This approach enables better feature extraction and visibility, making it ideal for marine research, underwater photography and robotic vision applications. DeepSeaView enhances image details while preserving natural textures, ensuring a more accurate and visually appealing representation of underwater scenes.

How it works ?


Underwater image enhancement using the Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) involves a series of processing steps to restore clarity, contrast and color in degraded underwater images. The process begins with White Balancing, which corrects the unnatural color cast caused by light absorption underwater, restoring a more natural color tone. Next, Normalization is applied to scale pixel intensity values, ensuring better brightness and contrast distribution. To remove unwanted noise and preserve important details, a Median Filter is used, effectively smoothing the image while maintaining edges. Further improvement is achieved through Luminance Adaptive Contrast Enhancement (LACE), which dynamically adjusts brightness and contrast based on local luminance variations, enhancing visibility in darker areas without overexposure. Finally, the Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) is utilized to analyze frequency components of the image, allowing selective enhancement of essential features while reducing distortions. After processing, the Inverse DCT (IDCT) reconstructs the enhanced image, delivering a clearer, more visually appealing underwater scene.


Why Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) ?


The Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) is an effective technique for enhancing these images because it processes them in the frequency domain, allowing for better restoration and visibility improvements.


Frequency-Domain Processing: DCT converts the image into frequency components, helping separate important details from noise, making enhancement more effective.

Color Correction & Contrast Enhancement: Underwater images lose red light and appear bluish or greenish. DCT helps restore balanced colors and improves contrast.

Noise Reduction: By filtering high-frequency components, DCT removes unwanted distortions and enhances clarity.


Edge & Detail Preservation: Unlike traditional smoothing methods, DCT enhances image details while reducing artifacts, ensuring better object recognition.

Efficient Computation: DCT is widely used in image processing applications like JPEG compression, making it computationally efficient for real-time enhancement.
